4 Applications of surface integration

4.1 Force on a dam

Section 27.1 considered the force on a rectangular dam of width 100 m and height 40 m. Instead, imagine that the dam is not rectangular in profile but instead has a width of 100 m at the top but only 80 m at the bottom. The top and bottom of the dam can be given by line segments y = 0 (bottom) and y = 40 while the sides are parts of the lines y = 40 − 4 x i.e. x = 10 − y 4 (left) and y = 40 + 4 ( x − 100 ) = 4 x − 360 i.e. x = 90 + y 4 (right). (See Figure 20).

Figure 20

Thus the dam exists at heights y between 0 and 40 while for each value of y , the horizontal coordinate x varies between x = 10 − y 4 and x = 90 + y 4 . Thus the surface integral representing the total force i.e.

I = ∫ A 1 0 4 ( 40 − y ) d A  becomes the double integral I = ∫ 0 40 ∫ 10 − y 4 90 + y 4 1 0 4 ( 40 − y ) d x d y

which can be evaluated as follows

I = ∫ 0 40 ∫ 10 − y 4 90 + y 4 1 0 4 ( 40 − y ) d x d y = 1 0 4 ∫ 0 40 ( 40 − y ) x 10 − y 4 90 + y 4 d y = 1 0 4 ∫ 0 40 ( 40 − y ) ( 90 + y 4 ) − ( 40 − y ) ( 10 − y 4 ) d y = 1 0 4 ∫ 0 40 ( 40 − y ) ( 80 + y 2 ) d y = 1 0 4 ∫ 0 40 3200 − 60 y − y 2 2 d y = 1 0 4 3200 y − 30 y 2 − 1 6 y 3 0 40 = 1 0 4 ( 3200 × 40 − 30 × 4 0 2 − 1 6 4 0 3 ) − 0 = 1 0 4 × 208000 3 ≈ 6.93 × 1 0 8 N

i.e. the total force is just under 700 meganewtons.

4.2 Centre of pressure

A plane area in the shape of a quadrant of a circle of radius a is immersed vertically in a fluid with one bounding radius in the surface. Find the position of the centre of pressure.

Figure 21

Note: In subsection 6 of Section 27.1 it was shown that the coordinates of the centre of pressure of a (thin) object are

x p = ∫ A x y d A ∫ A y d A and y p = ∫ A y 2 d A ∫ A y d A

∫ A y d A = ∫ 0 π 2 ∫ 0 a r 2 sin θ d r d θ = ∫ 0 π 2 1 3 r 3 sin θ 0 a d θ = 1 3 a 3 ∫ 0 π 2 sin θ d θ = 1 3 a 3 − cos θ 0 π 2 = 1 3 a 3 ∫ A x y d A = ∫ 0 π 2 ∫ 0 a r 3 cos θ sin θ d θ = ∫ 0 π 2 1 4 r 4 cos θ sin θ 0 a d θ = 1 4 a 4 ∫ 0 π 2 sin θ cos θ d θ = 1 4 a 4 1 2 sin 2 θ 0 π 2 = 1 8 a 4 ∫ A y 2 d A = ∫ 0 π 2 ∫ 0 a r 3 sin 2 θ d r d θ = ∫ 0 π 2 1 4 r 4 sin 2 θ 0 a d θ = 1 4 a 4 ∫ 0 π 2 sin 2 θ d θ = 1 4 a 4 ∫ 0 π 2 1 2 1 − cos 2 θ d θ = 1 8 a 4 θ − 1 2 sin 2 θ 0 π 2 = 1 16 π a 4

Then x p = ∫ A x y d A ∫ A y d A = 1 8 a 4 1 3 a 3 = 3 8 a and y p = ∫ A y 2 d A ∫ A y d A = 1 16 π a 4 1 3 a 3 = 3 16 π a .

The centre of pressure is at 3 8 a , 3 16 π a .
