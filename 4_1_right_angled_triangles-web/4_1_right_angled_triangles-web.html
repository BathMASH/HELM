Right-angled Triangles

HELM

Introduction

Right-angled triangles (that is triangles where one of the angles is 9 0 &compfn; ) are the easiest topic for introducing trigonometry. Since the sum of the three angles in a triangle is 18 0 &compfn; it follows that in a right-angled triangle there are no obtuse angles (i.e. angles greater than 9 0 &compfn; ). In this Section we study many of the properties associated with right-angled triangles.

Prerequisites

  • have a basic knowledge of the geometry of triangles

Learning Outcomes

  • define trigonometric functions both in right-angled triangles and more generally
  • express angles in degrees
  • calculate all the angles and sides in any right-angled triangle given certain information
